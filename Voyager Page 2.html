The Voyager Spacecraft

Voyager Today

- Upon the completion of their respective planetary exploration missions, both received mission extensions in 1989 to continue on and explore interstellar space
- Voyager 1 is now the most distant man-made object ever, crossing the heliopause on August 25th, 2012. It is currently 20.5 billion kilometers from Earth and travelling at over 38,000 miles per hour
- Both voyagers carry a golden record which contains information about humans and the Earth such as different languages, images, and even a interstellar map of Earth's position in the Milky Way should Voyager ever reach another intellegent civilization
- Currently represents the furthest man has ever gone, and will remain the most distant manmade object for the foreseeable future. It’s a testament to the longevity of human ingenuity and engineering - It will continue to function until 2025, when the radioactive decay generator running the probe's electronics dies
